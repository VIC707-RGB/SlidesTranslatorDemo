Slide 18:
Title: Where does Agile come from?
Contents:
- In 1956, Herbert D Benington described a process of development at a conference on advanced programming methods for digital computers. In 1970, Winston Royce wrote an article entitled: “Managing the Development of Large Software Systems”. These ideas formed the basis of what we know as the The Waterfall Model. Agile Development uses the stages from the Waterfall Model, but addresses its inadequacies.

Slide 19:
Title: What is The Waterfall Model?
Contents:
- The Waterfall Model is a sequential development model. It insists on each stage of the development process to be completed before moving on to the next step.

Slide 22:
Title: What is DSDM?
Contents:
- Dynamic Systems Development Method (DSDM) Agile Project Framework (2014 Onwards) It an Agile Project Delivery Framework that delivers the right solution at the right time. It is applicable to any kind of project. Key characteristics are: Focused on business benefit;On-time and in budget;Quality and rigour;Incremental and Collaborative.
